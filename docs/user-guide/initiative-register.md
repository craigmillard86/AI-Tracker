# The AI initiative register

*Drafted by HAP-13 (the register list and creating an initiative). Progressing an initiative through stages, and the monthly "still current?" nudges, arrive with HAP-14/HAP-18. Keep in step with the in-app help content (FR-072).*

The register is the group's live list of AI initiatives — one place that replaces tribal knowledge about who is doing what with AI, where each piece of work sits on the AI-DLC maturity path, and how it maps to the Harris categories used for group reporting.

## Finding initiatives

Open **Register**. You get a table of initiatives with, for each one: its business unit, Harris category, current stage, AI-DLC level, a RAG status, the customers it touches, and when it was last updated.

- **Search** by name or description — the box filters as you type.
- **Filter** by business unit, category, stage, risk tier, AI-DLC level, or dimension. Filters combine, so you can narrow to (say) "everything in my BU at level 2 that's amber".
- The list pages once there are more than 25 rows.

### Stale rows

An initiative that hasn't been updated recently is flagged in its **last update** column — amber after a week, red after two — with the number of days shown in text (not just colour). It's a prompt to check whether the record is still accurate; the flag itself doesn't change anything.

## Adding an initiative

If you're a **manager or a BU lead**, you'll see **New initiative**. You can add initiatives **for your own business unit**. (Leaders above BU level — group and portfolio leaders, executives — have a read-only view of the register; they don't create entries.)

Creating an initiative needs, at minimum:

- a **name**,
- the **business unit** (must be your own),
- a **Harris category**,
- an **AI-DLC level** (1, 2, or 3), and
- an **owner** (the person leading the work).

New initiatives start at the **Idea** stage. Moving an initiative along the stages comes in a later release.

> **"Other" category:** initiatives you classify as *Other* are tracked in the register but are **not** rolled into group-level Harris reporting — use it only when nothing else fits.

## Editing an initiative

You can edit an initiative if you're its **owner**, the person who **created** it, or the **BU lead** of its business unit. A BU lead can curate any entry in their own BU, but not initiatives in other BUs.

Two things are fixed once an initiative exists: its **business unit** and its **current stage** can't be changed through an edit (stage changes will have their own controlled flow in a later release).

## Managing one initiative

Open an initiative from the register to see its full detail page — its identity and technology, the value it delivers, its governance information, and its history.

### Moving through the stages

An initiative moves forward through stages: **Idea → Evaluation → Pilot → Production → Scaled → Retired**. Use the stage control to advance it.

- Stages only ever move **forward**. You can jump more than one stage at once (e.g. Idea straight to Pilot), but you can't move an initiative backward — if you got the stage wrong, record a new forward transition rather than editing history.
- **Retired is the end** — once an initiative is retired, it stays retired.
- Every move is kept as a dated, permanent entry in the **stage timeline** — the history is never rewritten, so you always have an accurate record of how the work progressed.

### The weekly update

For **active** initiatives (Evaluation through Scaled), post a short **weekly update** — a RAG status (green / amber / red) plus a one-line note. Idea-stage and Retired initiatives don't need weekly updates.

- The update refreshes the "last updated" date and appears at the top of the update trail (newest first).
- If an active initiative hasn't had an update in over a week, the detail page shows an **overdue** prompt. (New Idea-stage initiatives never show this — they're not on the weekly cadence yet.)

### Value and NR lines

Record the initiative's value as **NR (net-recurring) lines** — each line is Direct or Indirect, One-Time or Recurring, with an amount and description. These feed the group's Harris financial reporting.

- You can add and remove NR lines freely **until** a line has been locked into a submitted monthly Harris return. After that it can't be deleted (so a reported figure always keeps its underlying detail) — the line shows as locked.

### Governance and customers

The governance and risk fields (data sensitivity, approvals recorded elsewhere, technology used) are **for reporting only** — the register records them but **never blocks** your work on them. It is not an approval gate; approvals happen through your normal BU/group processes.

For initiatives in **customer-deployed** categories you can record a customer count; for internal-only categories that field doesn't apply.
