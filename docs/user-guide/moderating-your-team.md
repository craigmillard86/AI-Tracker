# Moderating your team's assessments

*For managers. Drafted by HAP-9 (FR-008–012, FR-063, FR-069); assembled into the full guide by HAP-21. Keep in step with the in-app help content (FR-072).*

Each month, after your team members complete their self-assessments, you review each one and record the **moderated score** — the score that counts for reporting and roll-ups. Your report then sees exactly what you decided and why.

## Your review queue

Open **Team → Reviews**. The queue lists each of your team members whose assessment you are the reviewer of for this cycle, with their status (still working / submitted / moderated) and a note if they are on leave.

- You see the people you manage directly. If one of your reports has left or is a contractor without a manager who can review, that review automatically escalates to you as the next responsible manager — you'll see it in your queue.
- If your queue is empty, either no one has submitted yet, or you have no reviews assigned this cycle.

## Reviewing one person

Open a person to see, side by side for each of the seven dimensions:

- **their self-score and evidence**, and
- **your score**, which starts at a sensible default (their self-score, or — if nothing has changed since last month — last month's moderated score, ready to carry forward).

Adjust any dimension where your view differs. Two rules to know:

- **Big differences need a reason.** If your score is **2 or more levels** away from the self-score, a comment is **required** — the form highlights the dimension and won't submit until you explain. This applies to carried-over differences too, not just ones you change by hand.
- **Both scores are kept.** Your report always sees their own self-score alongside your moderated score and the gap between them — moderation is transparent, not a silent override.

A **calibration** line shows the average gap across the dimensions, so you can sanity-check whether you're consistently harder or softer than the team's self-view.

## Submitting

Choose **Submit moderation**. Your scores become the moderated record and the card locks (read-only) so you don't submit twice. Your report can now open their result and see your scores, your comments, and where you diverged.

## If the cycle has closed

Once a cycle closes, moderation is locked. If you genuinely need to moderate after close, an administrator can grant a **late override** for that person — until then the form is read-only. Assessments left unmoderated at close are **auto-adopted** at the self-score and flagged as unmoderated in the reporting.
