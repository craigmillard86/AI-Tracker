# Emails the platform sends you

*Drafted by HAP-18 (FR-061/FR-037/FR-057). The platform emails you — and only emails; nothing is posted externally — to keep assessments and initiative updates on track.*

## If you have an assessment to complete

While an assessment cycle is open, if you haven't submitted yet you'll get a **reminder** with a direct link, by default **7, 3, and 1 days before the cycle closes**. Once you submit, the reminders stop — you won't be nagged for something you've already done.

## If you manage people

As the cycle nears its close, if any of your direct reports still haven't submitted, you'll get a short **escalation** listing who on your team is outstanding — the same people you'd see in your review queue, no one else.

## If you lead a business unit

Near cycle close you may get a **per-team summary** for your BU — **counts only** ("Team led by X: 2 not yet submitted"). It never names an individual or shows anyone's scores. You'll receive this only if you're set up as the BU's delegate lead; if your BU has no delegate lead configured yet, the summary simply isn't sent.

## If you own an AI initiative

If one of your active initiatives (Evaluation through Scaled) goes **more than 7 days** without a weekly update, you'll get a **nag** to post one. If it reaches **more than 14 days**, your BU lead gets an **escalation** listing the overdue initiatives. Initiatives still at Idea, or Retired, are exempt — they trigger nothing.

## When your assessment is moderated

When your manager completes moderation of your assessment, you get a short **notice** that it happened. The notice tells you the moderation is complete — it does not contain any scores.

## What we never put in an email

No email ever contains your assessment scores, a moderated value, or another person's individual data. Reminders say only that something is outstanding; BU summaries are counts. Everyone who receives a summary or escalation about a business unit is someone already entitled to see that unit's data.
